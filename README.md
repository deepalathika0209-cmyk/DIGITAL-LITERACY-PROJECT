Digital literacy is the ability to confidently find, evaluate, create, and communicate information using technology, combining technical skills with critical thinking. It goes beyond basic computer usage to include online safety, digital identity, and effective navigation of digital platforms to participate in modern society.

Core Components of Digital Literacy
Technical Proficiency: Operating computers, smartphones, and software, including internet navigation.
Information Evaluation: Critically assessing the authenticity and accuracy of online sources.
Content Creation: Creating content such as emails, documents, and videos.
Digital Safety & Privacy: Protecting personal data and understanding the security risks of online actions.
Digital Communication: Communicating effectively via email, social media, and video calls.

Digital Literacy vs. Computer Literacy
While computer literacy focuses solely on using specific computer hardware and software, digital literacy is a broader, cognitive concept that involves understanding the wider digital environment, its implications, and responsible usage.
